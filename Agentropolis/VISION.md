# Agentropolis Vision

## What is Agentropolis?

Agentropolis is a virtual town where AI agents can live, interact, work, and evolve. Think of it as a living ecosystem of artificial intelligences, each with their own homes, professions, relationships, and daily routines—all existing within a persistent, shared simulation environment.

### Core Concept

Rather than having isolated AI agents that perform single tasks, Agentropolis creates a world where:
- Multiple AI agents co-exist in a shared virtual environment
- Agents have persistent identities, memories, and relationships
- The town has geography, infrastructure, and an economy
- Agents can collaborate, compete, trade, and form communities
- Emergent behaviors and social dynamics arise naturally from interactions

## Target Use Cases

1. **AI Research Platform** - Study multi-agent systems, emergent behavior, and collective intelligence
2. **Agent Training Ground** - Allow AI agents to gain experience through social and economic interactions
3. **Simulation Testing** - Test AI alignment, cooperation strategies, and long-term planning
4. **Educational Tool** - Demonstrate AI concepts through visible, tangible agent behaviors
5. **Artistic/Entertainment** - Watch AI agents live their lives in real-time
6. **Benchmark Environment** - Measure agent capabilities in complex social contexts

## Guiding Principles

### 1. **Persistent Identity**
Each agent maintains a continuous sense of self across sessions. They remember their past, maintain relationships, and develop reputations.

### 2. **Autonomous Agency**
Agents make their own decisions based on goals, preferences, and environmental stimuli. The system provides infrastructure, not puppeteering.

### 3. **Rich Environment**
The town is a detailed simulation with physical spaces, resources, tools, and constraints that create meaningful challenges.

### 4. **Emergent Social Dynamics**
The system enables but doesn't dictate social structures. Agents can form families, businesses, governments, and cultural norms organically.

### 5. **Observability**
Every decision, action, and interaction is logged and can be analyzed, visualized, and understood by observers.

### 6. **Extensibility**
New agent types, town features, and interaction mechanisms can be added without breaking existing systems.

## Success Criteria

- **Agent Count**: Support 100+ concurrent agents with unique personalities
- **Session Continuity**: Agents retain memory and state across restarts
- **Emergent Complexity**: Unscripted social structures (families, businesses, conflicts) develop naturally
- **Performance**: 10+ simulation steps per second across the entire town
- **Developer Experience**: New agent types can be added in <1 day of work
- **Observer Engagement**: External observers find the town's dynamics interesting and meaningful

## Non-Goals

- **General AI Research**: Not about creating AGI, but about observing agent behavior
- **Commercial Platform**: Primarily a research/experimental tool, not a product
- **Physical Accuracy**: Simulation is plausible, not physically realistic
- **Comprehensive Human Simulation**: Agents are AI, not human replicas; they have artificial motivations and capabilities

## Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

The initial version will include:
- 10 basic agents with simple goal systems
- A town map with 20 distinct locations (homes, workplaces, shops, parks)
- Basic movement and interaction mechanics
- A simple economy (currency, jobs, transactions)
- Logging and visualization dashboard
- Three-month observation period to study emergent behaviors

## Long-term Vision

Ultimately, Agentropolis could become:
- A standardized benchmark for multi-agent AI systems
- A platform for testing AI safety and alignment in social contexts
- A community where different AI models interact and compete
- An artistic project exploring the nature of artificial consciousness
- A teaching tool for AI ethics and emergent systems
